## Style Prompt

Sharp GUI's introduction video should feel like a premium spatial-computing product reveal: dark cinematic canvas, Apple-like glass panels, precise technical typography, soft depth, and restrained blue light. The visual language should connect browser accessibility with 3D Gaussian Splatting: layered screenshots, floating point fields, device silhouettes, and clean data callouts. Motion should be smooth and confident, using focus pulls, blur crossfades, and subtle depth shifts rather than loud glitch effects.

## Colors

- Background: `#05070c`, near-black with a slight blue tint.
- Surface Glass: `rgba(238, 245, 255, 0.13)` for frosted UI layers.
- Primary Text: `#f5f7fb`, high-contrast Apple-style white.
- Secondary Text: `#b6c0cc`, cool readable supporting copy.
- Brand Blue: `#0071e3`, inherited from Sharp GUI.
- Cyan Light: `#6bdcff`, used sparingly for 3D scanning and spatial glow.
- Mint Depth: `#7ef0b4`, used only for generated-model depth accents.

## Typography

- Heading: `Noto Sans JP`, heavy weights for Chinese launch-style precision.
- Body/Data: `JetBrains Mono`, light to medium weights for technical readouts and captions.
- Use large video-safe type: 72px+ for major claims, 26px+ for subtitles, tabular numbers for metrics.

## Motion Rules

- Primary transition: blur crossfade / focus pull, 0.55s to 0.75s.
- Accent transition: overexposure flash only for the 3D reveal moment.
- Entrances must vary: focus, slide, scale, line draw, and staggered data ticks.
- Ambient motion stays slow and finite: drifting point fields, screenshot parallax, and gentle glass reflections.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use generic purple-blue gradients as the main visual identity.
- Do not use emoji icons, marketing-card grids, or stock-looking decorative blobs.
- Do not make the video feel like a web landing page; it should feel like a product film.
- Do not cover the actual UI screenshots with large text for long periods.
- Do not add narration; captions and music carry the structure.
